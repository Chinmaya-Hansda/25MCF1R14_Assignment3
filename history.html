History:

The National Institute of Technology (NIT) Warangal was founded in 1959 as the first Regional Engineering College (REC) in India, with its foundation stone laid by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on October 10, 1959. It was renamed the National Institute of Technology, Warangal, and granted Deemed University status in 2002. The institute is a leading institution for engineering and technology in India, known for its strong faculty, infrastructure, research, and alumni network.

Mission:

The National Institute of Technology Warangal (NITW) aims to become a global knowledge hub by providing high-quality education and research in technical fields, fostering innovation, and serving societal needs. The institute focuses on developing entrepreneurial and ethical future professionals through innovative, industry-aligned programs, while also contributing to knowledge and technological services for the benefit of society.
